This Cookie Policy explains how TechDev uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website and use our services. This policy provides you with clear information about these technologies and your choices.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and provide information to website owners.

Types of Cookies

Session Cookies

Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser. They help you navigate our website efficiently.

Persistent Cookies

Cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them. They remember your preferences and settings.

First-Party Cookies

Cookies set by TechDev directly on our website.

Third-Party Cookies

Cookies set by external services we use, such as analytics providers or advertising partners.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for several important purposes:

Essential Cookies (Required)

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as:

  • Security and authentication
  • Session management
  • Load balancing
  • Network routing
  • Access to secure areas

Can be disabled: No - These are essential for the website to work.

Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting anonymous information:

  • Number of visitors
  • Pages visited
  • Time spent on pages
  • Traffic sources
  • Error messages encountered
  • Device and browser information

Can be disabled: Yes - You can opt out of these cookies.

Functionality Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization:

  • Remembering your preferences
  • Language selection
  • Region selection
  • Theme preferences (dark/light mode)
  • Font size adjustments
  • Previous selections and settings

Can be disabled: Yes - But this may affect functionality.

Targeting and Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver relevant advertisements:

  • Track your browsing habits
  • Build a profile of your interests
  • Show relevant ads on other websites
  • Measure advertising campaign effectiveness
  • Prevent showing the same ad repeatedly

Can be disabled: Yes - You can opt out of these cookies.

5. Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use other similar tracking technologies:

Web Beacons (Pixels)

Small graphic images embedded in web pages or emails to track page views, email opens, and user behavior.

Local Storage

HTML5 local storage that allows websites to store data locally in your browser with no expiration date.

Session Storage

Similar to local storage but data is cleared when the browser tab is closed.

ETags

HTTP headers used to validate cached resources and can function similarly to cookies.

Fingerprinting

Collection of browser and device characteristics to create a unique identifier (we use this minimally and only for security purposes).

6. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

Cookie Control Center

Manage your cookie preferences at any time

Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Cookies

Mobile Devices

For mobile devices:

  • iOS: Settings → Safari → Block All Cookies
  • Android: Browser settings → Site settings → Cookies

Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of specific tracking:

  • Google Analytics: Install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Interest-based ads: Visit www.youronlinechoices.com or www.aboutads.info
  • Do Not Track: Enable "Do Not Track" in your browser settings
Important: Blocking or deleting cookies may affect your experience on our website. Some features may not function properly without cookies.

7. Consequences of Disabling Cookies

If you disable cookies, you may experience:

  • Inability to log in or maintain your session
  • Loss of personalized settings and preferences
  • Need to re-enter information on each visit
  • Reduced website functionality
  • Inability to use certain features
  • Less relevant content and advertising

8. Cookie Lifespan

Our cookies have different lifespans:

  • Session cookies: Deleted when you close your browser
  • Short-term cookies: 24 hours to 7 days
  • Medium-term cookies: 1 month to 6 months
  • Long-term cookies: 1 year to 2 years
